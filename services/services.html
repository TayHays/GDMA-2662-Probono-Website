Neuropsychology

Neuropsychological testing is an important component of the assessment and treatment of traumatic brain injury, dementia, neurological conditions, and psychiatric disorders. It is also used to examine the effects of toxic substances and medical conditions on brain function.

Concussion Testing

Concussion testing includes ImPACT (Immediate Post-Concussion Assessment and Cognitive Testing), an online neurocognitive assessment. It also includes baseline testing and post-injury testing which are used together to determine if a patient can safely return to physical activity.

EMDR for Trauma & PTSD

Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing, or EMDR is a structured therapy which helps clients reduce the vividness and emotions associated with traumatic memories.

CBT

Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy, or CBT, is a form of psychotherapy that treats problems and boosts happiness by modifying dysfunctional emotions, behaviors, and thoughts. CBT aims to identify harmful thoughts, assess whether they are an accurate depiction of reality, and if they are not, employs strategies to challenge and overcome them.
